1. What is phishing?
Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers pose as legitimate institutions to trick individuals into providing sensitive data. In the provided document, the attacker poses as a "Bank Security Team" and demands the user "verify your identity"  to steal credentials.

2. How to identify a phishing email?
You can identify phishing by looking for specific red flags, all of which are present in this sample:
Urgency: The subject line screams "A Urgent" and threatens suspension "within the next 12 hours".
Generic Greetings: It uses "Dear Customer"  instead of the recipient's name.
Suspicious Links: The URL secure-your-bank-account.com  does not match the sender's domain.
Grammar Errors: The subject line uses incorrect grammar ("A Urgent").

3. What is email spoofing?
Spoofing is the act of forging an email header so the message appears to come from a trusted source. In this case, the sender uses the address support@secure-bank-update.com to look official, even though the signature claims to be from "National Banking Group Pvt Ltd".

4. Why are phishing emails dangerous?
They are dangerous because they can lead to:
Credential Theft: The email directs users to a login page (/login) to harvest passwords.
Malware Infection: The email includes an attachment Account_Verification_Form.pdf, which could contain malicious code.
Financial Loss: The text threatens that "permanent closure will occur"  to scare users into giving up control of their funds.
